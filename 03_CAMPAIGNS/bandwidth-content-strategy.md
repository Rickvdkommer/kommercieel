# "Bandwidth" Content Strategy — 5 Open Loop Ideas

Goal: Long-form content that increases time-on-brand. Each piece bridges from a common problem to the Offer Doc. The reader should feel like they discovered the answer themselves.

---

## 1. "I Audited 12 SaaS Companies' Outbound Setups. Here's What's Broken."

**Format:** LinkedIn article or Loom walkthrough (10–15 min)

**The Hook:** "I looked at how 12 early-stage SaaS companies run outbound. Not one had a proper sending infrastructure. Here's the exact checklist I used and what I found."

**The Bridge:** Walk through the most common mistakes — sending from primary domain (killing deliverability), no warmup, no enrichment (just bought lists), single-channel (email only), no pipeline automation (everything manual in CRM). For each mistake, briefly explain the fix. End with: "If you want to see what a properly engineered outbound system looks like end-to-end, I wrote a full spec."

**Open Loop:** The reader now knows everything that's wrong with their setup but doesn't have the architecture to fix it. The Offer Doc is the architecture.

**Why It Works:** Founders reading this will check their own setup against the list. Self-diagnosis creates urgency without you having to manufacture it.

---

## 2. "The Actual Cost of Doing Outbound Manually (I Did the Math)"

**Format:** LinkedIn post or short article with a breakdown table

**The Hook:** "Your SDR costs $75K/year. But here's what you're actually spending when they do outbound manually."

**The Bridge:** Break down the hidden costs: SDR salary ($75K), time spent on research instead of selling (40% of their week = $30K wasted), bad data / bounced emails (tool costs + reputation damage), no pipeline automation (deals falling through cracks = lost revenue), ramp time without systems (3–6 months before productive). Total real cost: $120K+/year for an output that an engineered system + part-time operator can deliver for a fraction.

**Open Loop:** The math is undeniable, but the reader doesn't know how to build the alternative. The Offer Doc is the alternative, with fixed pricing that makes the ROI obvious.

**Why It Works:** Founders make decisions with spreadsheets. Give them a spreadsheet-level argument and the next step is logical, not emotional.

---

## 3. "How We Stole a Competitor's Audience and Got a 34% Reply Rate"

**Format:** Loom walkthrough (15–20 min) + gated template

**The Hook:** "I'm going to show you the exact workflow, screen by screen, of how we ran a Competitor Audience Steal campaign that generated 460 replies from 2,600 cold leads."

**The Bridge:** Full walkthrough: picking competitors, scraping LinkedIn engagement, filtering for ICP, enriching with Bitscale, writing the sequences, setting up the multi-channel cadence. Show real screens (anonymized). Show the reply rate data. At the end: "This is one workflow inside the GTM engine we build for clients. It runs monthly on autopilot after the initial setup."

**Open Loop:** The viewer sees the full workflow but realizes building the infrastructure to run it (domains, warmup, enrichment accounts, sequencing tools, CRM integration) is a project in itself. The Offer Doc packages that project.

**Why It Works:** This is your single best proof point right now. Turn it into bingeable content. Gate the template behind a DM or community join to capture leads.

---

## 4. "What Happens After You Build a GTM Engine (Month 1 vs Month 6)"

**Format:** LinkedIn article or narrative post

**The Hook:** "Month 1 of a new outbound system is ugly. Here's what it actually looks like — and why most companies quit too early."

**The Bridge:** Honest timeline. Month 1: learning phase, low volume, testing sequences, adjusting ICP criteria. Feels like nothing is working. Month 2–3: data starts compounding, you know which sequences work, reply rates stabilize, first qualified meetings. Month 4–6: the engine is humming — fresh lists monthly, sequences optimized, pipeline predictable, your SDR/AE is talking to qualified prospects instead of hunting. Paint the picture of what "the other side" looks like using real operational details (not vague promises).

**Open Loop:** The reader now wants the Month 6 outcome but realizes they'd need to survive months 1–3 without quitting or breaking the system. The Offer Doc removes that risk — we build it, we optimize the first 8 weeks, we hand it off running.

**Why It Works:** Manages expectations (builds trust) while making the long-term outcome feel inevitable. The gap between "I could do this" and "I want someone to get me past the hard part" is exactly where the Offer Doc sits.

---

## 5. "The GTM Stack I'd Build With $500/Month (And the One I'd Build With $2,000/Month)"

**Format:** LinkedIn carousel or article with two-tier breakdown

**The Hook:** "If I had to build a B2B outbound system from zero today, here's exactly what I'd buy at two budgets."

**The Bridge:**

**$500/month stack:**
- 3 sending domains ($3/month)
- Instantly ($97/month — sending + warmup)
- Apollo free tier (data)
- LinkedIn Sales Nav ($99/month)
- HubSpot free CRM
- Manual enrichment workflow (2 hours/week)
- Total: ~$200/month + your time

**$2,000/month stack:**
- Everything above, plus:
- Bitscale ($X/month — automated enrichment)
- Dripfy ($X/month — LinkedIn automation)
- Clay or Waterfall enrichment
- Slack integration + pipeline automation
- Total: ~$500/month tools + $1,500 for someone to run it

Walk through what each tool does and why. Be specific. Name names. Show the workflow.

**Open Loop:** The reader now has the shopping list but not the assembly instructions. They know WHAT to buy but not HOW to connect it all into a system that actually runs. The Offer Doc is the assembly.

**Why It Works:** Giving away the stack builds massive trust ("this person isn't hiding anything"). The complexity of integration becomes self-evident, which positions the done-for-you build as the logical next step.

## Distribution Notes

- **LinkedIn:** Primary channel. Post from Rick's personal profile (not company page). Thought leadership format.
- **GTM Legends community:** Share early, get feedback, create discussion threads.
- **Gating strategy:** Gate the Competitor Audience Steal template and the Loom walkthroughs behind DM or community join. Everything else is open.
- **Cadence:** 1 piece per week. Rotate between the 5 formats. Repurpose long-form into shorter LinkedIn posts.
- **CTA on every piece:** Soft link to the Offer Doc. Never hard-sell. Frame as "here's the full spec if you want it."
