# Google Ads Strategy — Notes from Rishi (Google)

**Source:** Conversation with Rishi, Google Advertising Campaign Manager
**Date:** March 2026
**Context:** Rishi manages Google's own advertising campaigns (Google Workspace, Cloud, etc.). He reviewed our Element Human ad strategy ($5K/month budget, B2B niche) and gave hands-on advice.

---

## 1. Marketing Funnel — KPIs by Stage

### Awareness
| Format | Best KPIs |
|--------|-----------|
| Display | Viewability, Time on Screen |
| Video | Video Completion Rate, AWOC (Active Viewable and Complete), Cost per View (CPV) |

**AWOC** = the user both completed the video AND it was viewable on screen (not scrolled past). This is the gold standard for video awareness.

### Consideration
| Format | Best KPIs |
|--------|-----------|
| Display | CTR, Cost per Click (CPC) |
| Video | CTR, CPC |

If driving to landing pages: **Cost per Landing Page Visit** is the best metric (closely mirrors CTR).

### Acquisition
- **CPA** (Cost per Acquisition) — primary
- **ROAS** (Return on Ad Spend) — if measuring value
- Track multiple conversion events (floodlights/pixels) at different funnel stages on the landing page

**Key insight:** Don't optimize toward the final conversion event immediately. Start by optimizing toward an earlier action (e.g., landing page visits), then progressively move to deeper actions (e.g., "Meet with us" click → form submit) as data accumulates.

---

## 2. Retargeting — The Most Important Tactic

Retargeting is BY FAR the most effective acquisition driver. Build your retargeting lists from awareness campaigns.

### Frequency Caps (Rule of Thumb)
- **Max 3x per day** (viewable impressions)
- **Max 5x per week**
- **Ideal frequency:** 2–4x per week for driving users further down funnel

### Frequency Formula
```
Frequency = Impressions / Reach
```

### Retargeting List Strategy
Create separate audience lists based on engagement depth:
1. **Landing page visitors** (broadest list)
2. **Interacted with page elements** (scrolled, clicked)
3. **Scrolled past 50%**
4. **Spent 10+ seconds on pricing section**
5. **Clicked "Meet with us" / CTA button** (highest intent, smallest list)
6. **Form submitters** (final conversion)

Run each list in separate ad groups. Test look-alikes of each. Switch off underperformers.

### Minimum Traffic for Retargeting
- **1,000 unique visitors/month** before retargeting ads make sense
- Below that, focus on organic growth first

## 3. Budget Strategy ($5K/month)

### Rishi's Honest Take
> "If this was my business with $5K/month, I would NOT go heavy on paid ads. I'd focus on organic first and build my seed list."

### Recommended Approach for $5K Budget
1. **LinkedIn Thought Leadership Ads** — best ROI for B2B at this budget (1.6x higher ROAS than standard ads)
2. **Google Search Ads** — demand capture for people actively searching (competitors, brand measurement, etc.)
3. **Influencer/Sponsored Posts** — pay LinkedIn niche influencers directly (not through LinkedIn Ads platform) for sponsored posts
4. **Retargeting** — only once hitting 1K+ monthly visitors

### Budget Allocation Warning
- $5K spread across multiple platforms = too thin to generate meaningful data
- Better: **concentrate on 1–2 platforms max**
- $5K/month ≈ $170/day — barely enough for meaningful testing
- Consider: Month 1 on Platform A, Month 2 on Platform B → compare, then consolidate

## 4. Campaign Setup Best Practices

### Campaign Structure
```
Campaign Level (controls budget)
├── Prospecting Campaign
│   ├── Ad Group: Broad Match Keywords
│   ├── Ad Group: Exact Match Keywords
│   └── Ad Group: Phrase Match Keywords
└── Remarketing Campaign
    ├── Ad Group: Site Visitors
    ├── Ad Group: YouTube Users
    └── Ad Group: High-Intent Actions
```

**Rule:** Separate Prospecting and Remarketing into different campaigns because you want separate budget control.

### Ad Group Strategy
- Each ad group = one targeting tactic
- Start simple: **same broad audience across all ad groups, different keywords per group**
- Categorize keywords into groups: competitor keywords, product keywords, problem/solution keywords
- Don't test too many variables at once — you're testing nothing if everything changes
- Use same keywords in both prospecting and remarketing campaigns (copy-paste)

### Keyword Strategy
- Use **Keyword Planner** in Google Ads for research (can also input competitor URLs)
- Start with a mix of **broad, phrase, and exact match** — test which performs best
- Create separate ad groups per match type within the same campaign
- **Long-tail keywords** = cheaper bidding + higher intent (more specific searchers)
- Look for: **low competition, low CPC** — monthly search volume doesn't matter much
- Add **negative keywords** to exclude irrelevant traffic

### Bidding Strategy (Critical Sequence)
1. **Weeks 1–3:** Use **Maximize Conversions** (no target CPA) — let the system learn
2. **After ~30 conversions:** Check your actual CPA in reports
3. **Then:** Switch to **Target CPA** based on your observed CPA
4. Adjust target CPA gradually — if actual CPA is $12 and target is $16, lower target to ~$11-12

**Never start with Target CPA on a fresh campaign — the system needs data first.**

### CPA Floodlight Strategy
Start with a broad conversion event (landing page visit) as your CPA goal, then narrow:
- Landing page visit → CTA click → Form start → Form submit

## 5. Campaign Health Indicators

### Signs of a Healthy Campaign
- Even daily budget pacing (spending consistently each day)
- Frequency between 2–4x per week
- CTR around 1.5%+ (good benchmark)
- CPC between $5–$10 (Google's own campaigns)
- Enough scale/reach for the system to optimize

### Signs of an Unhealthy Campaign
- Inconsistent daily spend → not enough scale or targeting too tight
- Stuck in "Learning Phase" for more than 2 weeks
- CPC above $40 (our keyword planner showed this — very high)
- Frequency too high (>5x/week = ad fatigue)
- CPMs very high for your niche

### Benchmarks
- Good CTR: ~1.5%+
- Good CPC: $5–$10 (varies by industry)
- Use **ComScore** or ChatGPT for industry CPM/CPC benchmarks as a starting point
- After first campaign: **always compare to your own historical data**, not industry benchmarks

## 6. Audience Targeting on Google Ads

### Available Targeting Options
- **In-market segments** — people actively researching your category
- **Affinity audiences** — people with long-term interests
- **Detailed demographics** — job function, company size
- **Custom intent** — based on search behavior
- **Lookalike audiences** — based on your retargeting lists
- **Site visitors** / YouTube users (remarketing)

### For Account-Based Marketing (ABM)
- Google Ads **cannot target specific companies** like LinkedIn can
- Best Google approach: target decision-maker demographics (CFOs, CTOs, CMOs at SMBs)
- For company-specific targeting: **use LinkedIn** instead
- Don't go super niche on Google to start — budget constraint means you need broader reach

### Audience Testing Approach
1. Start with **broad audience + specific keywords** (keep audience constant)
2. Once keywords are optimized, THEN test different audience segments
3. Create separate ad groups for different audience sets
4. Don't change both keywords AND audiences between ad groups (too many variables)

## 7. Creative & Pre-Planning

### Rishi's 40/50 Rule
> "40–50% of campaign success is just having great creative. The pre-planning and creative matter more than campaign settings and audience selection."

### Creative Best Practices
- Platform dictates format: Instagram = video > images
- Put **people's faces** in content — especially leadership/founders
- **Don't sell in first touchpoint** — tell stories, be authentic
- Test different creatives, see what resonates
- For LinkedIn: organic posts with real engagement > polished ad creative
- For Search: no creative needed — just headlines, descriptions, sitelinks

### Organic-First Creative Testing
1. Post organic content from CEO's personal profile
2. Track which posts get the most engagement
3. Boost the winners as paid Thought Leadership Ads
4. This gives you proven creative before spending ad budget

---

## 8. Landing Page Strategy

### Conversion Tracking Setup
- Install pixels/floodlights for EACH step of the landing page funnel
- Track: page visit → element interaction → CTA click → form start → form submit
- Always track ALL events even when optimizing for one — see where drop-offs happen

### Landing Page Design
- **One clear CTA** — don't overwhelm with options
- Example from Google Workspace: single "Get Started" button, leads to form
- Form should be simple: business name, email, basic info
- Use UTM parameters to track which campaigns/keywords drove the visit

---

## 9. Reporting & Optimization

### Dashboard Columns (Essential Metrics)
- Clicks
- Impressions
- CTR
- Cost (spend)
- Conversions
- CPA
- Frequency
- Budget pacing

### Optimization Cadence
- **Week 1–2:** Learning phase — don't change anything
- **Week 2–3:** Review initial data, pause worst performers
- **Week 3+:** Set target CPA, start narrowing
- **Ongoing:** Week-over-week improvements as the benchmark

### Key Rule: One KPI Focus
> "Choose ONE singular KPI before you start. Everything else is secondary. If two ads have similar primary KPIs, use a secondary metric as tiebreaker. If both are working, just keep both on."

## 10. Naming Conventions

### Google's Format
```
[Campaign ID] - [Product] - [Effort Type] - [Region] - [Language] - [Device] - [Media Format] - [Tactics] - [Creative Type]
```

Example: `WS-DR-NA-US-EN-Desktop-SEM-BKWS-Text`

- **BKWS** = Brand Keywords
- **SKWS** = Standard Keywords (non-brand)
- **DR** = Direct Response (lower funnel)

Pick a convention and stick with it — gets confusing fast without one.
